About

I'm a third-year undergraduate computer science student at Princeton University. I grew up in Baltimore and went to Park School.

This summer, I will intern in San Francisco at Judicata, an early stage startup building a legal research engine.

Last summer, I worked in the data infrastructure group at Facebook in Menlo Park, CA, where I fell in love with the suburbs of Silicon Valley.

I spent the summer before that working as a research programmer in Marius Usher's psychology lab at Tel Aviv University. The lab studies computational models of decision-making.

I spent the three summers before that programming in various labs at Johns Hopkins University, including a stint at Ernst Niebur's lab at the Mind/Brain Institute developing a simulator for biological neural networks.
